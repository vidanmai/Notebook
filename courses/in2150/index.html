Installed base

It's not a 'thing' but a concept, a lens... and 'relational'. Information infrastructures are shaped by the installed base.
The installed base is sociotechnical: technology + social + organizational structures and regulations (and more).
What is the Installed Base?

It depends on the case you are studying and it depends on the change process you want to focus on.

Questions to ask are:

What is the installed base?
How does it influence their design decision?
How is the installed base influencing the way the infrastructure is evolving?

Things that can contribute to a weak installed base is:
Security concerns: the infrastructure doesn't have appropriate levels of security required by law.
There are too many diverse solutions(?)
The user experience is too complicated and fragmented.

Coordination mechanisms (Mintzberg)

Mutual adjustment
- Coordination of work is made possible by a process of informal communication between people conducting inderdependent work.

Direct supervision
- Coordination is achieved by one individual taking responsibility for the work of others

Standardization
- of work processes
Coordination is made possible by specifying the work content in rules or routines to be followed. Coordination occurs before the acitivity is undertaken.
- of work outputs
Coordination is obtained by the communication and clarification of expected results. The individual actions required to obtain a goal are not prescribed.
- of worker skills
Coordination is reached through specified and standardised training and education.

ICT as support tool

Les Gasser (1986) "The Integration of Computing and Routine Work"

He studied "... how computing fit into the work ecology within the organizations".
Howd do people in complex organizations integrate computing into their work?
Gasser: Primary Work vs Articulation Work

Primary Work
Primary work is not the work of using the computer.
Computing is a resource for other tasks - computing supports the primary work.

Articulation Work

"The work to make it work"

The coordination of lines of work.
E.g: documentation wor in healthcare
E.g.: preparation for work in meetings

Gasser: How is work organized?

Task (oppgave)
- A task has an aim/goal, it is carried out by some person or group, who has access to some resources, it takes place over time and in some place.

Task chain (oppgavekjede)
- Each task is part of a larger system of tasks.
- A sequence of tasks

Production lattice (Produksjons-'nettverk')
-Task chains intersect and must be coordinated for the production of work.
- A complex coordinated structure of intersecting task chains.

Gasser: How is computing integrated into work?

There are three types of adaptation work:

Fitting
- The activity of changing computing or changing the structure of work to accomodate for computing misfit.

Augmenting
- Undertaking additional work to make up for misfit.

Working around
- Intentionally using computing in ways for which it was not designed or avoiding its use and relying on alternative means of accomplishing work.
